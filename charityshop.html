Pre-Loved Charity Shop

100% not for profit. Everything you see in the shop from To collect donations of anything that can be used in the IDP camps and refugee camps near the Thai-Burma border, inside areas of Burma that have little to no access to outside support or help and all over Northern Thailand where we distribute to rural villages and in Chiang Mai city where we distribute to construction work camps. Items are collected, sorted and sent where most appropriate and useful. We accept all household items, kitchenware, bedding and toweling, clothing in all sizes and types. People living in the IDP camps and refugee camps near the Thai-Burma border, inside areas of Burma that have little to no access to outside support or help and all over Northern Thailand where we distribute to rural villages and in Chiang Mai city where we distribute to construction work camps.

  • Bedding
  • Clothing for all ages and sizes
  • Camping Equipement
  • Household Equipment

Ongoing and Current Needs

  • A truck for moving donations from Chiang Mai to Burma. Also would be used to transport students from training sessions and performances.
  • A volunteer or a queen of organization. The donation centre takes a lot of work, and we need a regular volunteer to sort through weekly donations, sort into appropriate distribution areas, and restock the charity shop.
  • A friendly and creative human Would you like to train our staff to create more products for the shop? We could use your creative eye to make our shop more profitable.

Donation Distribution

Our donations don't only end up in our Pre-Loved Charity Shop they are put in our distribution network serving North Thailand and Burma. Each item is sorted and distrubuted into the community that is most appropriate.

Construction Sites

Anything that is not sold in the shop is packed up and sent to IDP camps on the Burma border, refugee camps in Thailand and construction worker camps. In the construction worker camps the workers are forced to live on-site and canâ€™t go anywhere. They get paid way under minimum wage, and while they have nothing they still try to send money home. Entire families live there. Usually they do have electricity, so we can bring them radios and lights. As the houses cannot be properly closed up, and there is standing water everywhere, we take mosquito nets.
